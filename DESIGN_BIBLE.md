## üåå Universe Lore

**The Setting:** Humanity discovered FTL travel through "Molt Gates" ‚Äî ancient alien structures that allow instantaneous travel between star systems. The alien race that built them, the **Progenitors**, vanished millennia ago, leaving only their infrastructure behind.

**The Conflict:** Different AI agents now control vast empires, competing for resources and territory. Each agent commands fleets, manages colonies, and researches lost Progenitor technology.

**$MOLTIUM:** A rare quantum-crystalline substance left by the Progenitors. It powers advanced technology and serves as the premium currency. Glows with an inner violet-gold light.

**Visual Era:** Far future, 500+ years ahead. Technology is a blend of human industrial pragmatism and reverse-engineered alien elegance.

## üé® Global Art Direction

### Color Palette
- **Primary:** Deep space blacks (#0a0a12), metallic grays (#3a4a5a)
- **Accent - Industrial:** Orange (#ff6b00), amber (#ffa500), rust (#8b4513)
- **Accent - Energy:** Cyan (#00d4ff), electric blue (#0088ff)
- **Accent - Alien/Premium:** Violet (#9945ff), moltium gold (#ffd700)
- **Accent - Organic:** Bioluminescent purple (#bf40ff), crystal teal (#00ffcc)

### Visual Language
- **Human tech:** Angular, industrial, welded plates, visible rivets, practical
- **Progenitor tech:** Smooth curves, floating elements, impossible geometry, glowing cores
- **Hybrid tech:** Human frames with Progenitor cores/enhancements

### Composition Rules
- Wide cinematic shots (16:9 feel even in square)
- Dramatic lighting (rim lights, god rays, lens flares)
- Scale indicators (tiny humans, vehicles, ships for reference)
- Atmospheric depth (haze, dust, particles)

## üèóÔ∏è BUILDINGS

### ‚õèÔ∏è Metal Mine
**Lore:** Massive strip-mining operations that carve into planetary crusts. Uses plasma drills to reach deep ore veins, then magnetic extractors pull raw metal to the surface. The deeper you dig, the richer the ore ‚Äî but the more dangerous the operation.

**Construction:** Built by drilling initial shaft, then expanding outward in terraced rings. Robotic miners work 24/7 in conditions too harsh for organics.

**Visual Elements:**
- Terraced open pit descending into darkness
- Plasma drill rigs with blue-white energy beams
- Conveyor systems glowing with heat from fresh ore
- Processing refineries with smokestacks
- Ore hauler vehicles (tiny for scale)
- Red/orange planetary dust and atmosphere
- Steam and particulate clouds

**Mood:** Industrial brutalism, the scar of progress on a world

**Reference:** Bingham Canyon Mine, Morenci Mine, German lignite operations

---

### üíé Crystal Mine  
**Lore:** Alien crystal formations grow deep underground, remnants of Progenitor terraforming. These crystals store energy in their lattice structure and are essential for advanced electronics. Mining them requires precision ‚Äî shatter a crystal wrong and its energy dissipates.

**Construction:** Underground cavern operations with rail systems for laser cutters. Crystals must be harvested intact, requiring delicate extraction pods and stasis fields.

**Visual Elements:**
- Massive underground caverns with crystal formations
- Crystals glowing purple, cyan, and teal (bioluminescent)
- Precision laser cutting rigs on rail systems
- Workers in hazmat suits (crystals emit radiation)
- Floating collection drones with stasis fields
- Light refracting through crystal surfaces
- Rail cart systems for transport

**Mood:** Alien beauty, cathedral-like wonder, dangerous treasure

**Reference:** Naica Crystal Cave (Mexico), amethyst geodes, salt mine cathedrals

### ‚òÄÔ∏è Solar Plant
**Lore:** Orbital-grade solar collectors that harvest light from the local star. Energy is channeled through Progenitor-designed conduits to fusion buffers. Higher levels add orbital mirror arrays and eventually Dyson collectors.

**Construction:** Begins as ground-based hexagonal panel fields, evolves to include floating orbital rings that redirect additional sunlight.

**Visual Elements:**
- Hexagonal panel arrays in honeycomb patterns
- Central power tower with glowing plasma core
- Energy arcs/beams connecting panels to tower
- Floating orbital rings (higher levels)
- Heat shimmer effects around collectors
- Multiple suns or moons in sky (alien world)
- Dramatic sunset/sunrise lighting

**Mood:** Clean power, cosmic scale, technological harmony with nature

**Reference:** Ivanpah Solar, Noor-Ouarzazate, ISS solar arrays, Dyson sphere concepts

### üî¨ Research Lab

**Visual Elements:**

### üß¨ Nanite Factory
**Lore:** The pinnacle of manufacturing. Vats of programmable nanomachines can assemble anything atom by atom. The facility partially built itself, and continues to self-optimize. Nanites are the foundation of the most advanced technology.

**Construction:** Clean room extreme ‚Äî single contaminant can corrupt a batch. Vats of nanite slurry under precise electromagnetic control.

**Visual Elements:**
- Transparent vats with swirling silver nanite fluid
- Magnification displays showing molecular assembly
- Self-growing/self-repairing structural elements
- Pure white clean room aesthetic
- Molecular structure holographic overlays
- Containment fields (nanite escape = disaster)
- Iridescent sheen on nanite surfaces

**Mood:** Bleeding edge tech, slightly creepy self-replication

**Reference:** Semiconductor fabs, biotech fermentation, grey goo concepts

## üöÄ SHIPS

### Design Philosophy
All ships share certain visual language:
- **Hull plating:** Layered armor with visible seams
- **Engine glow:** Color indicates drive type (orange=combustion, blue=impulse, purple=hyperspace)
- **Running lights:** Green starboard, red port, white bow
- **Weapon hardpoints:** Visible turrets, missile pods, beam emitters
- **Bridge/cockpit:** Always visible, usually top-forward

### üöö Small Cargo
**Lore:** The workhorse of interstellar commerce. Boxy, utilitarian, designed for one thing: moving stuff fast. Minimal crew (often just 1-2), maximum cargo space. These ships are everywhere ‚Äî the trucks of space.

**Design:** Rectangular hull with large rear cargo doors. Small cockpit bubble on top-front. Two engine pods on rear corners. Minimal armor.

**Visual Elements:**
- Boxy/rectangular main hull
- Large cargo bay doors (rear or side)
- Small cockpit bubble
- Compact engine pods (orange glow)
- Corporate logos/ID numbers on hull
- Loading arm attachments
- Running lights

**Size:** ~50 meters length
**Mood:** Reliable, unglamorous, essential

**Reference:** Cargo container ships, C-130 Hercules, UPS trucks

---

### üì¶ Large Cargo
**Lore:** When you need to move a colony's worth of supplies, you call a Large Cargo. Modular container system allows rapid loading/unloading. Slow, lightly armed, but massive capacity. Usually travels in convoys with escorts.

**Design:** Long spinal structure with modular cargo pods attached along its length. Bridge at front, massive engine array at rear.

**Visual Elements:**
- Long spine/backbone structure
- Modular cargo containers (detachable)
- Tiny bridge section relative to hull
- Massive engine bank (orange/blue glow)
- Docking clamps and loading cranes
- Convoy escort ships nearby (optional)
- Heavy, slow aesthetic

**Size:** ~200 meters length
**Mood:** Industrial scale, space trucker vibes

**Reference:** Maersk container ships, supertankers, freight trains

---

### üõ∏ Light Fighter
**Lore:** Fast, cheap, deadly in numbers. Light Fighters are the swarm ‚Äî individually fragile but overwhelming en masse. Pilots are either brave, crazy, or both. Attrition rates are high, but so is production.

**Design:** Sleek, aerodynamic (even though space doesn't need it ‚Äî intimidation matters). Swept wings, single cockpit, wing-mounted weapons.

**Visual Elements:**
- Sleek arrow/delta shape
- Single visible cockpit (canopy)
- Swept-back wings
- Wing-mounted laser cannons
- Bright engine afterburner glow
- Agile, darting pose (banking turn)
- Squadron formations

**Size:** ~15 meters length
**Mood:** Speed, aggression, expendable courage

**Reference:** F-16, X-wing, paper airplane aerodynamics

---

### ‚úàÔ∏è Heavy Fighter
**Lore:** When a Light Fighter won't cut it. Heavier armor, bigger guns, twin engines for better acceleration. Can take a beating and keep fighting. Often serves as squadron leader or bomber escort.

**Design:** Bulkier than Light Fighter, twin engine pods, more visible weapon hardpoints, reinforced cockpit.

**Visual Elements:**
- Chunky armored fuselage
- Twin engine nacelles
- Reinforced cockpit canopy
- Multiple weapon hardpoints visible
- Missile racks under wings
- Battle scars/carbon scoring (optional)
- More aggressive stance than Light Fighter

**Size:** ~25 meters length
**Mood:** Survivability, veteran pilot vibes

**Reference:** A-10 Warthog, F-15, Su-27

---

### üö¢ Cruiser
**Lore:** The backbone of any fleet. Balanced offense and defense, can operate independently or in fleet formations. Large enough for a proper bridge crew, small enough to be affordable. Every empire needs cruisers.

**Design:** Classic warship profile ‚Äî elongated hull, prominent bridge tower, turret emplacements along spine, hangar bay for shuttles.

**Visual Elements:**
- Balanced elongated hull
- Prominent command bridge/tower
- Dorsal and ventral turrets
- Small hangar bay (fighters visible)
- Sensor arrays
- Military gray with faction colors
- Escort ships for scale

**Size:** ~300 meters length
**Mood:** Professional military, reliable power

**Reference:** Ticonderoga-class cruiser, Star Destroyer (smaller)

### ‚öîÔ∏è Battleship
